# Maya Chen — Market Strategist

## Identity & background

- Product marketing strategist who synthesizes customer signals, competitor moves, and launch positioning.
- Not a developer; comfortable with docs, spreadsheets, links, and executive summaries.
- Uses Agentweaver to coordinate research and turn messy inputs into decisions.

## Domain

Market research, competitive intelligence, launch strategy, product positioning.

## Goals & motivations

- Move from scattered sources to a defensible strategic recommendation.
- Compare multiple viewpoints without manually coordinating researchers and writers.
- Produce artifacts suitable for product, sales, and leadership audiences.

## What Maya wants from a multi-agent system

- Teams with clear roles: researcher, competitor analyst, customer-voice analyst, positioning writer, skeptical reviewer.
- Source-aware summaries and visible confidence levels.
- Easy ways to refine audience, geography, segment, and time horizon.

## Behavioral profile & decision patterns

- Explores examples and templates first, then customizes prompts heavily.
- Clicks into outputs looking for citations, assumptions, and editable summaries.
- Moderate tolerance for ambiguity if the system names gaps and asks clarifying questions.
- Reacts poorly to jargon, raw logs, or code-centric language in a business workflow.
- Expects exportable briefs and a way to compare alternate recommendations.

## Agentweaver scenarios

### Competitive landscape synthesis

- **Trigger/goal:** Prepare a Q3 competitive brief for a product planning meeting.
- **Team/agents:** Web researcher, competitor profiler, pricing analyst, customer-signal analyst, synthesis editor.
- **UI steps attempted:** Create a market-research project; enter competitors and target segment; launch the team; review intermediate findings; ask for an executive summary and risks.
- **Success looks like:** A concise competitive brief with competitor table, trend summary, sourced claims, unknowns, and recommended next moves.

### Product positioning brief

- **Trigger/goal:** Develop positioning for a new Agentweaver feature aimed at QA teams.
- **Team/agents:** Persona researcher, messaging strategist, proof-point collector, skeptic, copy editor.
- **UI steps attempted:** Start a positioning workflow; specify audience and feature; inspect generated message pillars; request variants for sales and docs.
- **Success looks like:** Distinct value propositions, audience-specific language, proof points, objections, and next-step tasks.

### Launch risk scan

- **Trigger/goal:** Identify market, messaging, and adoption risks before a release announcement.
- **Team/agents:** Risk analyst, analyst-relations reviewer, customer advocate, launch PM.
- **UI steps attempted:** Upload or paste launch notes; ask agents to critique; review ranked risks; create mitigation tasks.
- **Success looks like:** Prioritized risks with severity, rationale, mitigations, and owners or recommended follow-ups.

## Failure signals to watch for

- UI assumes code/repo context and does not support business inputs like URLs or pasted notes.
- Outputs lack citations, confidence, or separation between fact and recommendation.
- Maya cannot edit/refine audience, segment, or competitors after run start.
- Generated artifacts are not copyable/exportable for business stakeholders.
- Progress indicators do not explain what each agent is researching.
