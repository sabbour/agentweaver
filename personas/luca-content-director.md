# Luca Moretti — Content Director

## Identity & background

- Runs a small content team producing blogs, social posts, launch pages, and executive narratives.
- Strong editorial instincts; limited patience for developer-focused setup.
- Uses Agentweaver as a virtual writing room with specialists that critique and refine drafts.

## Domain

Content strategy, editorial production, brand voice, campaign planning, localization handoff.

## Goals & motivations

- Turn one content brief into coordinated assets for multiple channels.
- Preserve brand consistency while letting specialists challenge weak claims.
- Shorten review cycles with structured critique and revision history.

## What Luca wants from a multi-agent system

- Teams with roles such as strategist, drafter, SEO reviewer, brand editor, legal reviewer, localization reviewer.
- Easy iteration: ask for a stronger intro, shorter social post, or more technical proof.
- Artifacts that are easy to copy, compare, and route for approval.

## Behavioral profile & decision patterns

- Starts with examples, brand notes, and desired audience rather than technical configuration.
- Clicks through generated drafts, comments, and variants; compares alternatives side by side.
- High tolerance for creative ambiguity; low tolerance for losing edits or voice constraints.
- Reacts to errors by asking for a smaller asset or regenerating a section.
- Expects previews and version history more than logs.

## Agentweaver scenarios

### Campaign content pipeline

- **Trigger/goal:** Generate launch-week content from a feature brief.
- **Team/agents:** Campaign strategist, long-form writer, social copywriter, SEO analyst, brand editor.
- **UI steps attempted:** Create content project; paste feature brief and audience; select campaign workflow; review generated asset list; request channel-specific drafts.
- **Success looks like:** Blog outline, launch email, social snippets, SEO notes, review checklist, and clear next edits.

### Executive blog post room

- **Trigger/goal:** Produce an executive thought-leadership draft with rigorous critique.
- **Team/agents:** Ghostwriter, industry analyst, skeptical editor, proof-point researcher, tone reviewer.
- **UI steps attempted:** Enter thesis and audience; run writing-room scenario; inspect critique; ask for revised draft.
- **Success looks like:** A polished draft with argument structure, supporting evidence, alternative headlines, and noted weak claims.

### Localization readiness pass

- **Trigger/goal:** Prepare campaign assets for international localization.
- **Team/agents:** Localization reviewer, cultural-risk reviewer, glossary manager, concise-copy editor.
- **UI steps attempted:** Select existing generated assets; request localization review; inspect flagged idioms and region-specific concerns.
- **Success looks like:** A readiness report with problematic phrases, glossary terms, transcreation notes, and required edits.

## Failure signals to watch for

- Generated assets disappear or cannot be compared after iteration.
- The UI forces technical concepts like repos, branches, or sandboxes into content-only workflows.
- Luca cannot preserve brand voice or mark preferred drafts.
- Review comments are not attached to the relevant asset or section.
- Export/copy behavior corrupts formatting, links, or headings.
