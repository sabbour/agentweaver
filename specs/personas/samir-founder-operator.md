# Samir Patel — Founder Operator

## Identity & background

- Early-stage startup founder juggling strategy, hiring, fundraising, customer discovery, and operations.
- Comfortable making decisions with incomplete information but needs leverage and focus.
- Uses Agentweaver as an operating staff: analysts, skeptics, writers, and planners.

## Domain

Startup operations, strategic planning, fundraising preparation, team design, operating cadence.

## Goals & motivations

- Turn scattered founder context into clear priorities and artifacts.
- Stress-test plans before presenting to investors or the team.
- Convert strategy into concrete work without losing the rationale.

## What Samir wants from a multi-agent system

- Fast setup and reusable teams for recurring founder workflows.
- Explicit trade-offs, risks, decision memos, and task breakdowns.
- A way to move from brainstorm to execution plan in one workspace.

## Behavioral profile & decision patterns

- Starts broad, then quickly narrows to the most actionable path.
- Clicks anything that promises templates, examples, or one-click team assembly.
- High tolerance for ambiguity if momentum is visible; low tolerance for setup friction.
- Reacts to errors by switching scenarios or asking for a rough draft anyway.
- Expects concise outputs and clear next actions more than exhaustive detail.

## Agentweaver scenarios

### Board memo prep

- **Trigger/goal:** Prepare a monthly investor update and board discussion memo.
- **Team/agents:** Business analyst, narrative writer, risk skeptic, metrics reviewer, action-item scribe.
- **UI steps attempted:** Create founder-ops project; enter metrics and narrative bullets; run board-memo team; review summary and risks; ask for final memo.
- **Success looks like:** Investor-ready memo with wins, misses, asks, metrics caveats, risks, and board discussion prompts.

### Hiring plan workshop

- **Trigger/goal:** Decide which roles to hire next under budget constraints.
- **Team/agents:** Org designer, finance analyst, product lead, engineering lead, skeptic.
- **UI steps attempted:** Provide goals, budget, current team, and constraints; launch hiring-plan workflow; compare options.
- **Success looks like:** Prioritized roles, trade-offs, timing, risks, interview loop suggestions, and decision rationale.

### Operating cadence design

- **Trigger/goal:** Create a lightweight weekly operating rhythm for a growing team.
- **Team/agents:** Operations designer, team-health reviewer, meeting-minimizer, metrics advisor.
- **UI steps attempted:** Enter team size and pain points; request cadence design; inspect proposed meetings, metrics, and artifacts.
- **Success looks like:** Weekly cadence, meeting purposes, async updates, decision log rules, and improvement checkpoints.

## Failure signals to watch for

- Setup requires too much domain modeling before producing value.
- Outputs are verbose and do not surface decisions, asks, or next actions.
- Samir cannot save reusable founder workflows or rerun them with updated context.
- The UI hides trade-offs or gives a single recommendation without alternatives.
- Generated plans cannot be converted into tasks or follow-up runs.
