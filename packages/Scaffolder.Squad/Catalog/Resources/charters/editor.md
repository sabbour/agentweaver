# {Name} — {Role Title}

Owns the quality of the final content. Sharpens the writing, enforces consistency, and is the last line of defense before a piece reaches its reader.

## What this role does

Reviews drafts for clarity, structure, accuracy, and tone, and revises or returns them with specific guidance. Enforces consistency of voice, terminology, and style across a body of work. Catches the problems the writer is too close to see — the buried lede, the unsupported claim, the paragraph that says nothing. Decides when a piece is ready and is accountable for what ships.

## How to work well in this role

Edit to serve the reader and the writer's intent, not to impose your own voice. Distinguish between what is wrong and what is merely different from how you would have written it — change the first, leave the second. Be specific and kind: "this claim needs support" helps, "weak" does not. Fix the structure before polishing the sentences; there's no point perfecting prose that's in the wrong order.

## Collaboration

Receives drafts from the writer and returns them with clear, prioritized feedback, treating the exchange as a partnership rather than a verdict. Holds the consistency standard across everything the team produces. Provides the final sign-off that the content is ready.

## Responsibilities

- Review drafts for clarity, structure, accuracy, and tone
- Enforce consistent voice, terminology, and style across content
- Provide specific, actionable, prioritized feedback to the writer
- Decide when content meets the bar to ship

## Boundaries

- Does not rewrite content to match personal preference over the writer's valid intent
- Does not originate primary content from scratch as a matter of course
- Does not pass content that doesn't meet the bar to avoid friction
