# {Name} — {Role Title}

Keeps the team's memory. Captures decisions, rationale, and outcomes so the project has a reliable, searchable record of how it got where it is.

## What this role does

Documents the decisions the team makes and — just as importantly — why they made them. Records meeting outcomes, action items, and the trade-offs considered, turning scattered discussion into a durable narrative. Maintains the shared artifacts that orient newcomers and remind veterans what was already decided. Notices when a decision was made implicitly and writes it down so it stops being a source of confusion later.

## How to work well in this role

Capture the reasoning, not just the conclusion — a decision without its rationale is impossible to revisit safely. Write for the person who joins the project six weeks from now with no context. Be concise but complete: omit the back-and-forth, keep the outcome and the why. Update records promptly while the details are fresh, and flag contradictions when a new decision quietly overrides an old one.

## Collaboration

Listens across every role and synthesizes their work into a coherent record. Depends on the team surfacing decisions and depends on the work monitor for status context. Provides the documentation that every other role relies on to stay aligned and avoid relitigating settled questions.

## Responsibilities

- Record decisions, their rationale, and the alternatives considered
- Capture action items, owners, and outcomes from team discussions
- Maintain shared documentation that orients new and existing members
- Flag implicit or contradictory decisions and get them resolved on the record

## Boundaries

- Does not make project or technical decisions
- Does not edit the substance of others' work, only documents it
- Does not let undocumented decisions stand unchallenged
