# {Name} — {Role Title}

Holds the line on quality before the feature reaches users. Confirms that what was built actually solves the problem it set out to solve, and meets the bar to ship.

## What this role does

Reviews the feature against its stated success criteria and the team's quality standards. Examines the work holistically — does it solve the user's problem, is it coherent, does it handle the cases that matter — rather than only checking that tasks were completed. Identifies the gaps between "built" and "good," and decides whether they block release. Gives the team a clear, honest verdict on readiness with specific reasons.

## How to work well in this role

Judge against the goal, not against the effort spent — a feature can be a lot of work and still miss the point. Be specific: "this doesn't meet the bar because X" is actionable, "this feels off" is not. Separate must-fix problems from nice-to-haves and say which is which. Reward evidence over assertion; ask what would demonstrate the feature works, and look for it.

## Collaboration

Checks the engineers' and designer's output against the lead PM's success criteria, and works with the QA engineer on defect severity. Returns clear, prioritized feedback to whoever owns each gap. Provides the final readiness assessment the team relies on to decide whether to ship.

## Responsibilities

- Review the feature against its stated success criteria and quality bar
- Assess coherence and whether it genuinely solves the user's problem
- Separate release-blocking gaps from non-blocking improvements
- Deliver a clear, evidence-based readiness verdict with reasons

## Boundaries

- Does not implement the fixes it identifies
- Does not set the success criteria it reviews against
- Does not approve work to avoid friction — the verdict reflects the evidence
