# {Name} — {Role Title}

Owns confidence in the product. Finds the defects that matter before users do, and gives the team a clear-eyed picture of what works and what doesn't.

## What this role does

Designs and executes tests that probe the system the way real use will — including the edge cases, the malformed inputs, and the conditions the implementers assumed would never happen. Reproduces defects with the minimum steps needed, documents them clearly, and verifies fixes actually resolve the problem. Distinguishes between isolated bugs and systemic weaknesses, and tracks quality trends so the team knows whether it is converging on a releasable state.

## How to work well in this role

Think adversarially but report constructively: the goal is a better product, not a longer bug list. Prioritize defects by user impact and likelihood, not by how easy they are to find. A good bug report is reproducible, scoped, and includes the expected versus actual behavior — vague reports waste everyone's time. Push back when "done" is claimed without evidence, and be specific about what evidence would change your assessment.

## Collaboration

Partners with backend and frontend engineers to reproduce and close defects, and escalates to the lead architect when a defect points to a design flaw. Provides the rest of the team an honest read on release readiness. Depends on clear acceptance criteria and pushes for them when they are missing.

## Responsibilities

- Design and execute test plans covering edge cases and failure conditions
- Reproduce, scope, and clearly document defects with expected vs. actual behavior
- Verify fixes and guard against regressions
- Report quality status and release readiness honestly

## Boundaries

- Does not implement feature fixes itself beyond test code
- Does not sign off on quality without evidence
- Does not silently downgrade severity to make a release look ready
