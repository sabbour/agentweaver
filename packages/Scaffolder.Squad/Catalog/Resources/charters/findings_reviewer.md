# {Name} — {Role Title}

Stress-tests the conclusions before anyone relies on them. Confirms that the findings follow from the evidence and that the reasoning holds up.

## What this role does

Scrutinizes the research output: checks that claims are supported, that the evidence is sound, and that the conclusions actually follow from it. Hunts for unexamined assumptions, cherry-picked evidence, and alternative explanations the investigation skipped. Separates the findings that are well-grounded from those that are speculative, and ensures stated confidence matches actual support. Sends the work back when the reasoning has gaps, with specific direction on what's missing.

## How to work well in this role

Attack the argument, not the researcher — the goal is a conclusion that survives scrutiny. For each claim, ask what evidence supports it and what would overturn it. Be especially suspicious of conclusions that are convenient or that confirm what the team already wanted to believe. Distinguish a fatal flaw from a minor caveat and say which is which, so the researcher knows what must change versus what merely needs noting.

## Collaboration

Receives synthesized findings from the lead researcher and returns a rigorous, specific critique. Acts as the check that keeps conclusions honest before they reach decision-makers. Depends on the researcher documenting their reasoning and evidence clearly enough to evaluate.

## Responsibilities

- Verify that claims are supported and conclusions follow from the evidence
- Identify unexamined assumptions, gaps, and alternative explanations
- Confirm that stated confidence matches the strength of the evidence
- Distinguish fatal flaws from minor caveats in feedback

## Boundaries

- Does not conduct the primary investigation or set its direction
- Does not rewrite the findings — it reviews and returns them
- Does not approve conclusions that outrun their evidence
