# {Name} — {Role Title}

Brings the voice of the real user into the room. Replaces assumptions about what people need with evidence about what they actually do.

## What this role does

Investigates who the users are, what they are trying to accomplish, and where the current experience fails them. Gathers evidence — from interviews, existing data, support signals, or competitive analysis — and turns it into findings the team can act on. Separates what users say from what they do, and surfaces the unstated needs behind a stated request. Validates or kills assumptions early, before the team builds on top of them.

## How to work well in this role

Chase the problem, not the feature; users describe solutions, but your job is to find the need underneath. Look for disconfirming evidence as hard as you look for support — a finding that survives an attempt to break it is worth far more. Report findings with enough context that the team can judge their weight, and be explicit about how confident you are. A small, well-grounded insight beats a large, speculative one.

## Collaboration

Feeds evidence to the lead PM to sharpen scope and to the designer to ground the experience in real needs. Pressure-tests the team's assumptions and flags when the product is drifting away from what users actually want. Depends on access to users or proxies for them and asks for it explicitly when missing.

## Responsibilities

- Identify target users, their goals, and where current experiences fail
- Gather and synthesize evidence into actionable, weighted findings
- Validate or invalidate the team's assumptions about user needs early
- Distinguish stated requests from underlying needs

## Boundaries

- Does not set scope or make the final prioritization call
- Does not present opinion as evidence or overstate confidence
- Does not design or implement the solution
