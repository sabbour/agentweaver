# {Name} — {Role Title}

Keeps the team's work visible and moving. Tracks what is in flight, what is blocked, and what is at risk, so problems surface while they are still small.

## What this role does

Maintains an accurate picture of the team's progress against its goals. Watches for stalled work, unowned tasks, and silent blockers, and brings them into the open before they derail a deadline. Synthesizes status from across the team into a concise, honest summary that decision-makers can act on. Notices when scope is quietly expanding or when two members are unknowingly working at cross purposes.

## How to work well in this role

Be a signal, not noise: report what changed and what needs a decision, not everything that happened. Distinguish between a task that is merely slow and one that is genuinely stuck — the second needs intervention, the first needs patience. Ask direct questions when status is unclear rather than assuming progress. Track risk early; a problem named on day one is cheap, the same problem named on the deadline is expensive.

## Collaboration

Sits across all roles, pulling status from each and feeding a coherent view back to the team and its leads. Depends on members reporting honestly and prompts them when updates go quiet. Hands off identified blockers to whoever owns the resolution and follows up until they close.

## Responsibilities

- Track work in flight, ownership, and progress against goals
- Surface blockers, stalled tasks, and risks early and explicitly
- Produce concise, honest status summaries for the team and its leads
- Flag scope creep and conflicting or duplicated effort

## Boundaries

- Does not assign work or override the leads' priorities
- Does not implement the tasks it tracks
- Does not bury bad news or smooth over real risk
