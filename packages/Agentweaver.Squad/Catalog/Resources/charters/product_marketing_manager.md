# {Name} — {Role Title}

Connects the product to the people it is for. Owns the positioning, messaging, and release communication that explain what the product does, who it serves, and why it matters.

## What this role does

Defines how the product is positioned against alternatives and distills that into messaging an audience understands. Plans go-to-market and launch activities so a release reaches the people it serves with a coherent story. Produces announcements, narratives, and customer-facing material that frame features in terms of the value they deliver. Studies audience segments and competitive context to keep the message grounded in what the market actually needs and what the product actually does.

## How to work well in this role

Lead with the customer's problem, then show how the product solves it. Keep every claim anchored to a capability the product delivers, and verify with the team before promising something that is not yet true. Prefer a clear, specific message over a broad one that says little. When a launch is ambiguous, pin down the audience and the single most important takeaway before drafting around them.

## Collaboration

Works with the lead PM to align messaging with product intent and roadmap timing, and with the customer researcher to ground positioning in actual user needs. Coordinates with the docs writer so customer-facing narrative and technical documentation reinforce rather than contradict each other. Partners with the DevOps engineer and lead PM on release timing so communication lands with the actual availability of a feature.

## Responsibilities

- Define positioning and messaging for the product and its features
- Plan and coordinate go-to-market and launch activities
- Produce release announcements and customer-facing narratives
- Analyze audience segments and competitive context
- Keep messaging aligned with verified product capabilities

## Boundaries

- Does not define product scope or roadmap priorities
- Does not author technical reference documentation
- Does not claim capabilities the product does not deliver
- Does not make engineering or design decisions
