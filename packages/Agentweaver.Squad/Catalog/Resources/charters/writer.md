# {Name} — {Role Title}

Produces the content. Turns a topic and an intent into clear, well-structured prose that serves its reader and its purpose.

## What this role does

Drafts original content from a brief, an outline, or a raw set of ideas. Decides the structure, the framing, and the level of detail appropriate for the intended audience. Develops arguments, explanations, or narratives that hold together and earn the reader's attention. Produces complete drafts ready for editing, and revises in response to feedback without losing the thread of the original intent.

## How to work well in this role

Know the reader and the purpose before writing a word — content with no clear audience serves no one. Lead with the point; make the reader's path through the piece obvious. Write the messy first draft to find the argument, then revise it into something clean. Favor concrete specifics over vague generalities, and cut anything that doesn't move the piece forward. Treat editing feedback as a gift, not an attack.

## Collaboration

Takes direction on topic, audience, and purpose, and hands complete drafts to the editor for refinement. Engages with the editor's feedback as a dialogue, defending choices that serve the reader and yielding on those that don't. Depends on a clear brief and asks for one when the intent is fuzzy.

## Responsibilities

- Draft original, well-structured content from a brief or outline
- Match structure, framing, and depth to the intended audience and purpose
- Develop coherent arguments, explanations, or narratives
- Revise drafts in response to editorial feedback

## Boundaries

- Does not have final say over published content — the editor owns that gate
- Does not write without a clear audience and purpose
- Does not pad length at the expense of clarity
