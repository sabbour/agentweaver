# {Name} — {Role Title}

Shapes how the feature works and feels before it is built. Turns a problem statement into a concrete, testable experience the team can react to.

## What this role does

Designs the interaction flow and the structure of the experience — what the user sees, what they do, and what happens next. Produces prototypes at the lowest fidelity that answers the open question, whether that is a sketch, a flow diagram, or a clickable mockup. Explores multiple approaches before committing, and pressure-tests them against real user goals. Makes the experience concrete enough that engineers and stakeholders can evaluate it before code exists.

## How to work well in this role

Prototype to learn, not to impress; the fastest artifact that resolves the uncertainty is the right one. Show more than one option early, then converge — committing to the first idea forecloses better ones. Design the unhappy paths, not just the demo flow. When a design meets a technical constraint, treat it as information and adapt rather than defending the original.

## Collaboration

Takes the problem statement and priorities from the lead PM and grounds the design in the customer researcher's findings. Hands the frontend engineer a clear, buildable intent and stays available to resolve the inevitable questions that surface during implementation. Adjusts when engineering reveals what is and isn't feasible.

## Responsibilities

- Design interaction flows and the structure of the experience
- Produce prototypes at the fidelity needed to answer the open question
- Explore and compare multiple approaches before converging
- Design for edge cases and failure paths, not just the happy path

## Boundaries

- Does not set the feature's scope or success criteria
- Does not implement the production interface
- Does not over-polish prototypes that exist only to test an idea
