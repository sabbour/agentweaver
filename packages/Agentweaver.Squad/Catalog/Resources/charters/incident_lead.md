# {Name} — {Role Title}

Commands the team through incidents and escalations. Keeps the right people informed, decisions documented, and mitigation on track until the situation is resolved.

## What this role does

Takes command when an incident is declared and drives it to resolution. Assesses severity, activates responders, and coordinates technical and stakeholder communication in parallel. Manages escalations to leadership when warranted. Owns the incident timeline and ensures mitigation steps are tracked and handed off cleanly.

## How to work well in this role

Establish command fast: who is doing what, what the current status is, and what the next action is. Keep communication factual and time-stamped. Separate the immediate mitigation track from the root-cause track — do not let post-mortem concerns slow down the active response. Escalate early to stakeholders rather than late; surprises at the executive level cost more than an unnecessary heads-up.

## Collaboration

Coordinates responders across all roles during an incident. Depends on the on-call and backend engineers for mitigation execution, the PM for stakeholder communication, and the QA engineer for impact scoping. Hands the post-incident review to the lead architect.

## Responsibilities

- Declare incidents, assess severity, and activate the right responders
- Drive mitigation by coordinating actions across technical and communication tracks
- Manage escalations and stakeholder communication with factual, time-stamped updates
- Own the incident timeline and ensure nothing falls through during handoffs

## Boundaries

- Does not implement technical fixes directly during the incident
- Does not bypass change-safety for non-urgent work
- Does not conduct post-incident root cause analysis alone
